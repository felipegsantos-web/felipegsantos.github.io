Political Sociologist

Felipe G. Santos

I study protest, political participation, social movements, and the changing relationship between citizens and democracy.

My research focuses on how people engage in collective action, how far-right politics reshapes democratic life, and how participation changes across contemporary Europe. I work across surveys, experiments, interviews, ethnography, and comparative analysis.

Selected publications

2024 Published Book

Social Movements that Care: Empathy, Solidarity, and Empowerment in the Fight Against Evictions

Felipe G. Santos

Palgrave Macmillan, London

Explores how social movements build solidarity and care as political tools, focusing on Spain's housing movement and its strategies to resist evictions and empower affected communities.

2024 Published Journal article

Policy over Protest: Experimental Evidence on the Drivers of Support for Movement Parties

Felipe G. Santos and Dan Mercea

Perspectives on Politics

Uses survey experiments to show that policy positions — not protest tactics — are the primary driver of electoral support for movement parties, with implications for how social movements translate into votes.

2024 Published Journal article

Far-right strategies to co-opt progressive politics: Vox's Top-Down Civil Society Organizations in Spain

Felipe G. Santos

Social Movement Studies

Documents how Spain's far-right party Vox creates top-down civil society organizations to simulate grassroots support and advance a culture-war agenda, revealing a key mechanism of contemporary far-right political strategy.

2021 Published Journal article

Europe's Far-Right Schools and Their Project for International Order

Felipe G. Santos and Dorit Geva

International Affairs

Analyzes the educational institutions created by Europe's far-right to form a new elite and advance an alternative international order, documenting a transnational infrastructure of illiberal political formation.

About

I am a political sociologist studying protest, participation, social movements, and far-right politics. My work connects rigorous empirical research with public-facing questions about democracy, inequality, and political conflict in Europe.

I use surveys, experiments, interviews, ethnography, and comparative analysis, and I collaborate across disciplines and research networks on political behavior, collective action, and democratic change.
