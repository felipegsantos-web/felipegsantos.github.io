Research Fellow · Political Sociology

Felipe G. Santos

City St George's, University of London
Babeş-Bolyai University, Romania

I study social movements, political participation, and the far right in Europe. My work examines how citizens engage with politics beyond the ballot box — through protest, civil society organizations, and movement parties — and how far-right actors co-opt or contest these forms of collective action.

I am currently a Research Fellow on the INTERFACED project (HORIZON, City St George's) and the TakePart project (NextGenerationEU, Babeş-Bolyai University).

Selected Publications

Book

Social Movements that Care: Empathy, Solidarity, and Empowerment in the Fight Against Evictions

Felipe G. Santos · Palgrave Macmillan · 2024

Explores how social movements build solidarity and care as political tools, focusing on Spain's housing movement.

Journal Article

Policy over Protest: Experimental Evidence on the Drivers of Support for Movement Parties

Felipe G. Santos and Dan Mercea · Perspectives on Politics · 2024

Uses survey experiments to show that policy positions — not protest tactics — drive electoral support for movement parties.

Journal Article

Europe's Far-Right Schools and Their Project for International Order

Felipe G. Santos and Dorit Geva · International Affairs · 2021

Analyzes the educational institutions created by Europe's far right to form a new elite and advance an alternative international order.

Under review

Digital Protest Repertoires and Political Participation in Post-Pandemic Europe

Felipe G. Santos, Dan Mercea, and Mathias Hoffmann

Examines how pandemic-era shifts in protest repertoires — toward online and hybrid forms — have reshaped patterns of political participation across European democracies.

Working paper

Authoritarian Nostalgia and Democratic Backsliding: Survey Evidence from Southern Europe

Felipe G. Santos

Uses original survey data to investigate the relationship between nostalgia for authoritarian regimes and declining support for democratic institutions in Portugal and Spain.

Forthcoming — Routledge

Methodological Pluralism in the Study of Political Participation

Felipe G. Santos and Claudius Wagemann

Book chapter contribution to Protest and Democracy (Routledge). Argues for methodological pluralism as a core principle in studying complex participation phenomena.
